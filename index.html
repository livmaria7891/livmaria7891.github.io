I'm passionate about two things: Technology and Education. As a former educator and future web developer, I spend my free time learning to code so I can build awesome applications that make high quality education more accessible to more people.

My Journey

..and some things I've learned along the way.

A teacher..

The three years I spent teaching special needs students in Tulsa, Oklahoma were just as amazing as they were challenging. I learned so much about how children think, the tools they need to learn, and how thinking critically can affect a persons’ life. On the flip side, I saw so many children fall through the cracks of an educational system that wasn’t suited to fit their needs or their learning style.

A tech newbie...

I got my start at Skillcrush and recently have been learning through Free Code Camp, where I’ve learned HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and JQuery. I have an affinity for clean, readable code and love dabbling in design. Since my humble coding beginnings, I’ve taken on a few freelance projects and recently started building basic web applications. I’m excited to get my hands dirty with more complex apps soon!

My goals...

When I first heard about Sugata Mitra’s “Hole in the Wall” project, I knew combining my love for technology with my passion for education was one of the most impactful things I could do with my career. Technology is such a powerful resource, but it’s so underutilized. I aspire to build high quality, accessible learning platforms to help close the gap between a student's ability and her potential.
